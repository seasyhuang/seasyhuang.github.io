Research

Currently, we're looking at how slow breathing affects blood pressure in young healthy individuals. This is important because, unfortunately, approximately one in three people worldwide (more than 1 billion people in 2010) are hypertensive; concerningly, less than half of those are aware of their status. Given that high BP is often asymptomatic, this condition left unattended and untreated is a major risk factor for more serious health conditions including but not limited to stroke, heart attack, and organ failure.

Our current research focuses on young healthy individuals because we need to better understand the physiology behind these mechanisms before we can apply our knowledge to clinical populations. Eventually, we plan to test hypertensives and women with PCOS.

My project looks at the physiological variables that could affect the blood pressure response to slow breathing. I can talk about my research forever, so just reach out if you want to hear more!
